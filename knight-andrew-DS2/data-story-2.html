The case for the Quarterback MVP: Who deserves the honors of the best NFL Quarterback in the 2016 season?

Who is the best quarterback of 2016?

By: Andrew Knight

Through the 2016 NFL season, many quarterbacks have made the case to be labeled as the best quarterback in the league. There certainly hasn’t been any shortage of competition this year. You have the “old-guys”: Drew Brees, Tom Brady, Ben Rothlisberger, Carson Palmer, Philip Rivers, and Eli Manning. You have the middle of the pack: Matt Ryan, Joe Flacco, Matthew Stafford, Cam Newton, Tyrod Taylor, and Aaron Rodgers. You have the young guns: Carson Wentz, Dak Prescott, Trevor Siemian, Blake Bortles, Marcus Mariota, Jameis Winston, Derek Carr, Russell Wilson, and Kirk Cousins. What goes into the most valuable quarterback?

In reality, there are four main statistical categories that go into making someone the best quarterback in the league.

Regular Season Statistics

This is probably the most obvious point of what makes a quarterback the best in the league. By regular season statistics, we are able to see a quarterback's passing yardage total, total number of touchdowns, number of completed passed, number of interceptions, and total quarterback rating. The data blow shows the TD/INT ratio among some of the top quarterbacks in the league.

The Quarterback’s Team Record

In order to be a good quarterback, you have to be able to lead your team to a successful winning season. On a football team, the quarterback is arguably one of the most important positions. If a team is doing bad, seven times out of ten fall it will fall on the quarterback. What makes a good quarterback is his ability to lead his team into the playoffs. One of the best traits a truly great quarterback must have is the ability to lead his team to victory during crunch time of a game. This proves that the quarterback can face adversity and preform under pressure.
